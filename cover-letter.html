Dear Sahil and the hiring team,

The most consequential part of this role is not choosing between client service and product management. It is preserving the context inside an enterprise request long enough to make the right product decision. The client needs a useful result now. The company needs a capability that becomes more coherent, not more fragmented, after delivering it.

That is the seam I have worked throughout my career. At Vape-Jet, I work directly across customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP, CRM, telemetry, production, quality, and AI-assisted workflows - turning scattered signals into prioritized work, cleaner handoffs, and reusable mechanisms. Through DudeWorth, I design AI-readiness frameworks and agentic workflow patterns that connect business value, structured context, human authority, evaluation, escalation, and governance. At Amazon, I led launch and execution in a high-intensity, customer-backward environment. Earlier, at Compunetics, I translated between engineering, manufacturing, quality, and high-consequence customers including DoD, CERN, Intel, and AMD.

I would bring a practical discipline to the role: treat every enterprise request like a query plan, not a ticket. Parse the customer intent, operating context, constraints, smallest proof, and reuse signal before the work becomes a roadmap commitment. Then make one explicit disposition - configure, prototype, productize, or decline - with a named owner and evidence standard.

READ CONTEXTOutcome, data, stakeholders, authority
BOUND THE WORKShared product versus legitimate variation
PROVE BEHAVIORSmallest test, evaluation, failure modes
RETURN LEARNINGPrimitive, playbook, rule, or explicit non-pattern

I created a role-specific candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/TalentPluto/. It includes the interactive Enterprise Query Plan, the evidence map behind my fit, and the 90-day cadence I would use to turn account learning into product leverage. The work is independently produced and does not guess the confidential partner's identity or internal process.

I would welcome a working conversation around a sanitized client request: the outcome, the context that matters, the smallest credible prototype, the evidence threshold, and what should return to the roadmap. I am deliberately interested in the hands-on scope - owning account outcomes, building with modern AI tools, and installing mechanisms that help a small team scale without losing judgment.
