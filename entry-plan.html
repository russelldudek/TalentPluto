Days 1-15

Read the account system

Earn context before changing the cadence.

  • Shadow active accounts, founder decisions, onboarding, customization, prototype reviews, and engineering handoffs.
  • Map the customer outcome, AI-search problem, data environment, workflow, stakeholders, decision owner, and required result.
  • Identify where account context currently lives, where it is compressed, and where it disappears.

Outputs: shared account brief; current-state request-to-product map; stakeholder and decision-right map; baseline for time-to-value, handoff completeness, rework, and support burden.

Days 16-30

Standardize the Enterprise Query Plan

Make invisible product decisions explicit.

  • Introduce one request record: intent, context, constraint, smallest proof, reuse signal, and disposition.
  • Define prototype minimums: user, data, authority, failure modes, evaluation, owner, expiry, and next decision.
  • Establish a weekly client-to-product review with the appropriate founder, product, and engineering participation.

Outputs: query-plan template; decision log; prototype evidence standard; client/product measurement categories.

Days 31-60

Prove one complete query plan

  • Select one representative enterprise request with clear client value and non-trivial product learning.
  • Build the smallest production-minded prototype; test data assumptions, human authority, failure behavior, evaluation, and adoption burden.
  • Deliver the immediate client result while capturing the reusable primitive and a complete product/engineering handoff.

Outputs: working prototype; evaluation record; explicit disposition; engineering handoff; learning returned to the roadmap and next account.

Days 61-90

Make the learning reusable

  • Convert the successful path into onboarding, configuration, prototype, and handoff patterns.
  • Prioritize productization using client value, repeatability, strategic fit, support burden, confidence, and time-to-learning.
  • Define what the role should automate, delegate, standardize, or stop doing as account volume grows.

Outputs: client-to-product playbook; evidence-ranked roadmap priorities; capacity and role-boundary view; 90-day learning memo.

90-day scorecard

Client outcomeTime-to-value, measurable result, adoption, executive confidence
Product leverageReusable primitives, multi-account applicability, roadmap confidence
Operating qualityHandoff completeness, rework, support burden, decision clarity
Learning speedTime from ambiguity to tested assumption and explicit disposition

Scale decisions by day 90

StandardizeRepeated high-confidence paths and handoffs.
AutomateLow-risk work with clear evidence and human review.
DelegateStable work with explicit decision rights.
StopLow-leverage exceptions and unclear commitments.
