The pressure behind the title

Three tensions define the actual work.

A small, high-growth team cannot choose between client success and product leverage. It has to deliver both through one operating mechanism.

01

Enterprise specificity vs. platform coherence

Customers pay for fit. The product wins when fit is expressed through bounded configuration and reusable primitives rather than uncontrolled forks.

02

Speed to prototype vs. engineering confidence

A prototype must answer a real decision: what works, what evidence exists, who owns the risk, and what is ready for an engineering handoff.

03

Founder intensity vs. scalable operating memory

Direct founder access accelerates learning, but the company compounds only when account context, product decisions, and results become reusable operating memory.

First 90 days

Earn trust, create visibility, then compound learning.

Days 1-15

Read the system

  • Shadow active accounts and founder decisions.
  • Map onboarding, customization, prototype, and handoff paths.
  • Define the shared account brief and decision vocabulary.
Days 16-30

Instrument the work

  • Establish account outcomes and evidence standards.
  • Create a client-delta register and roadmap decision log.
  • Baseline time-to-value, rework, and handoff completeness.
Days 31-60

Prove one loop

  • Run one account request through configure / prototype / productize / decline.
  • Ship a production-minded prototype with evaluation notes.
  • Capture the reusable primitive and owner.
Days 61-90

Make it repeatable

  • Install a weekly client-to-product review.
  • Prioritize roadmap by client value and reuse economics.
  • Turn the first proof into an onboarding and handoff playbook.
