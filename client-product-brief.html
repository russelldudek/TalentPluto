Parse the request before it becomes a roadmap commitment.

Preserve the customer outcome and operating context, define the smallest proof, and return the learning to the product decision.

Illustrative plan: attribution proof

Client language“Can you prove what AI search is changing for our pipeline?”A defensible executive decision, not a vanity report.
IntentDecide where AI-search visibility is influencing pipeline.Named business decision and owner.
ContextCRM stages, source taxonomy, visibility signals, executive owner.Data and workflow boundaries.
ConstraintCausal claims must remain inside the available evidence.Trust and communication boundary.
Smallest proofLink one visibility signal to one pipeline decision with an explicit evaluation method.Reduce uncertainty before commitment.
Reuse signalA bounded attribution primitive that can serve multiple accounts.Pattern, limits, and handoff criteria.
DispositionPrototype with bounded claims.Analytics owner + product owner.

Configure

The shared product already solves the need. Bound setup and measure time-to-value.

Prototype

An important uncertainty remains. Build the smallest test that can answer it.

Productize

The need repeats, the economics work, and the evidence supports a reusable primitive.

Decline

The work has weak leverage, unsafe ambiguity, or excessive support burden.

Required inputs

  • Customer outcome and decision
  • Workflow and data context
  • Authority and failure consequences
  • Current product coverage
  • Repeatability and support burden

Required outputs

  • One explicit disposition
  • Evidence and assumptions
  • Decision owner
  • Product/engineering dependencies
  • Client communication
  • Learning returned to the next account
