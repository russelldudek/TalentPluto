# Brand intelligence

## Identity

The campaign uses the supplied TalentPluto mark for nominative identification. The original uploaded image bytes are committed directly as `assets/brand/talentpluto-logo.jpg`; the 200 × 200 source is not traced, redrawn, recolored, cropped, or animated. Its SHA-256 is `5f18f4586982f5a324e57b197f43f482f30bc964a0a6945882ef75c2f60b89f2`.

The mark is visibly rendered in the site header, at the center of the client-to-product learning loop, and on each document route. The surrounding language identifies the experience as Russell Dudek's independent candidate vision rather than an official TalentPluto property.

## Color system

- TalentPluto blue: source-sampled from the supplied mark.
- White and dark ink: neutral supporting colors.
- Lime: candidate-original accent used only to signal learning, evidence, and decision return.
- Pale blue and cool gray: candidate-original supporting surfaces.

The employer-recognition layer remains blue and white. Candidate-original colors are subordinate to the role-derived operating system.

## Typography

The campaign uses broadly available humanist sans-serif fallbacks: Avenir Next, Segoe UI, Helvetica, and Arial. No proprietary font files are copied or committed.

## Role-derived visual system

The approved production concept is the Client-to-Product Translation System. A four-part decision record moves from account context to legitimate client delta, prototype evidence, and product disposition. Scenario selection changes the evidence threshold, human authority, recommended posture, and learning return.

The visual language uses the user-approved circular client-to-product learning loop in the hero, a horizontal translation spine in the interactive system, and restrained editorial evidence and entry-plan rails. The design was preserved during QA repair rather than replaced with a new metaphor. It is an independent candidate work product and does not imitate an official TalentPluto application or employee interface.
